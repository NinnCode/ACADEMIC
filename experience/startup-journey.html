My Journey in a Startup

I had the valuable opportunity to support a startup initiated by my brother, contributing both capital and my technical skills. My involvement went beyond just engineering; it was a hands-on lesson in the realities of building a business from scratch.

My Core Responsibilities

As a third-year university student at the time, my ability to be physically present at the startup's location in my hometown was limited. Therefore, my role was primarily focused on remote R&D, technical problem-solving, and financial support.

  • Securing Capital & Financial Contribution: I actively participated in securing funds to maintain our operations and fuel R&D initiatives. This included contributing personal capital and taking out a low-interest student loan to invest in the business.
  • R&D for a Cost-Effective Cup Printer: My task was to research existing printing machinery and conceptualize a new device that could deliver higher quality and efficiency at a lower manufacturing cost. This involved creating detailed design blueprints.
  • Developing an Automated Packaging Solution: I identified a significant bottleneck in the agricultural supply chain: the manual, time-consuming process of preparing Double-layer Fruit Protection Bags. I designed and built a working model of an automated machine to find a way to solve this problem, aiming to save farmers hours of labor.

The Journey: A Timeline of Milestones and Challenges

March 2024: Launch & Initial Resourcefulness
+ We officially launched by renting a space, hiring staff, and acquiring initial equipment. We successfully secured our first clients and began generating revenue.
-> Key Achievement: First income generated, initial client base established.
August 2024: Expansion Amidst Competition
+ We encountered equipment malfunctions and faced rising competition from new local printing services. In response, we relocated, brought on a new partner to increase capital, and expanded our team with a delivery driver.
-> Result: Successfully expanded our client base.
November 2024: Investment in Innovation
+ To stay competitive, I secured a low-interest student loan to fund further R&D on the cup printer design and to diversify our services by adding plastic bag printing.
January 2025: Business Turmoil
+ A critical turning point. Due to disagreements on business direction and financial difficulties, a key partner and our delivery driver departed to start their own venture. This event destabilized operations and, with limited capital, the path to realize my engineering concepts was no longer viable. Consequently, my direct involvement shifted from active R&D to a more observational role.
April 2025: The Business Ceases Operations
+ The business faced a lot of problems, My brother attempted to pivot the business by selling off equipment and exploring other avenues. However, persistent capital issues combined with the onset of Vietnam's rainy season, which led to a sharp decline in demand for both printed cups and double-layer Fruit Protection Bag. The drop in orders became unsustainable.

Outcome: Operations Halted

Faced with insurmountable financial pressure, we made the difficult decision to cease operations to address our debts. The startup has temporarily failed.

Personal Achievements & Lessons Learned

  • Although the business venture with my brother ended in failure, I owe the bank 120 million VND from a low-interest student loan used to support my startup and research. However, I have gained valuable lessons in both technical and business aspects. I developed concepts and designs for automated machines used in printing and fruit foam packaging, which are cost-effective and function efficiently. At the same time, I acquired knowledge about engineering, the market, and customer needs. I believe all these setbacks will become invaluable experiences and powerful motivation for me to grow and thrive in the future.
